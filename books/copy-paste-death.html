Manifesto

Copy, Paste, and the Death of Reason

In an industry drowning in frameworks, tutorials, and pre-written packages, this broadside is a surgical strike against the decay of true engineering.

Copy, Paste, and the Death of Reason is not a guide, course, or gentle reminder. It’s a war cry for those sick of the glucose era—where word developers assemble what they barely understand and call it mastery.

Hirschauer unpacks the rot festering beneath “move fast” dogma and invites engineers to sit with the uncomfortable questions hidden beneath shiny abstractions.

  • The tutorial addiction loop
  • Framework worship and abstraction overload
  • The myth of 10× engineering
  • The silence of undocumented systems
  • The terrifying rise of momentum without brakes

If you’ve ever stared at a blank file and felt panic—if you’ve ever felt like the only one asking why—this reckoning was written for you.
